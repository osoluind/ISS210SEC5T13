And Why It Matters

Portfolio

About

Countless women in the corporate world are suffering the cut of their paycheck while men are thriving off of unequal pay and more at the expense of these women. When enduring unjust treatment between the different genders. Gender inequality in the workplace is a large and rising issue in our society today. According to Pew Center Research, 42% of women in the workforce have experienced a wide variety of discrimination at work, including microaggressions, earning less wages, feeling ostracized or isolated, having worse treatment than their male counterparts, and more. We consistently see men getting promoted into higher roles as women are again and again denied these same roles. It isn’t because of some lack of competence that all women seemingly possess, it is because of discrimination and unconscious or conscious gender bias against women in the workplace. While it’s easy to say that this shouldn't be the case in our growing, diverse society, realistically, it is the case. Gender- bias discrimination isn't just evident in pay but is also shown through non-verbal actions, bonuses, promotions, security in jobs, and bias in group work. In the USA, the workforce is about 52.7% men and 47.2% women. Similarly, women have approximately an 18.5% wage gap for full-time workers compared to men who are employed the same. In our day and age, women deserve to be seen, not just because they are women, but because their degrees make them equally qualified for these roles that are being passed over to men who may not be. Equal opportunity is not a privilege, but a right that all women are entitled to regardless of gender. Moreover, 60% of women in Quebec, Canada work in the same four fields: retail, education, health care, and social assistance. When there are little to few options it doesn’t give very much occupational freedom that men have. They don’t have the same financial opportunities presented to them with equal representation. Therefore, this leaves thousands of different jobs in Quebec left to be run by men. Without differentiation in gender in occupations, we lack more than 20% of diversity. It has been proved that without gender diversity, men may be less likely to promote new innovation and services, and instead, focus on the ideation process. When talking about diversity in the workplace, it’s clear to see that it is more beneficial than having one, specific group of people. For instance, increased creativity, revenue, and productivity are all products of a diverse environment. Gender diversity brings in a larger scope of skills than if it were just one gender. Maybe the most important benefit is that gender equality yields “better results.” Thus, it’s a very important goal to work towards in the workplace. Some ways to increase women's positions in the workforce are verifying that processes for hiring people are free of gender biases. When having an open mind to all genders it can benefit a corporation in more ways than one. It can be a space for not only men, but open to women too with the right representation. Furthermore, when given the same rights and equal representation this can make the economical side improve as well. Additionally, requiring diversity training in the workplace, promoting an inclusive culture in corporations, and setting specific targets that increase women's numbers in leadership positions are all things that should be worked towards. These practices must be maintained however, otherwise little progress can be made.
